About Me

I focus on storytelling and social impact marketing. This campaign explores how visual hooks and emotional narratives can raise awareness about hidden poverty and encourage meaningful support for vulnerable communities.

The Poverty We Don’t See

This campaign challenges assumptions about poverty. Many people believe poverty has a clear appearance, but in reality it often exists quietly behind normal lives and working families.

Which one is poverty?

Three men sit at a table. Each appears troubled in different ways. The viewer naturally tries to guess who represents poverty. The uncertainty reveals that poverty does not have a clear face.

What does poverty really look like?

Poverty can feel like a void that people fall into silently. The black hole visual symbolizes how individuals can feel trapped and isolated by financial hardship.

"Need food to survive."

Behind statistics are real people. The crying ID card highlights the human reality behind economic struggle and food insecurity.

Ever wonder where your money really goes?

Many people hesitate to donate because they cannot see the impact of their contributions. The image of money disappearing into a black hole represents the lack of transparency that often discourages giving.

Transparency Builds Trust

Showing real campaign results helps build credibility and confidence in giving. When people can see the impact of their contributions, they are more willing to participate.
