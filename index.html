About Me

I’m a marketing professional focused on storytelling, social impact campaigns, and audience-driven messaging. My work explores how powerful visuals and clear narratives can raise awareness about overlooked issues and inspire action.

The Poverty We Don’t See

This campaign explores the hidden reality of poverty in modern society. Many people assume poverty has a clear appearance, yet millions of individuals struggle quietly behind normal lives and full-time jobs.

Which one is poverty?

Three men appear troubled in different ways. The viewer naturally tries to guess which one represents poverty. The uncertainty challenges the assumption that poverty has a recognizable face.

What does poverty really look like?

Poverty is often invisible. Many people struggling financially still appear to live normal lives. The cave symbolizes isolation and the hidden nature of hardship that many experience silently.

"Need food to survive."

Behind statistics are real human stories. This visual highlights the emotional reality faced by individuals who struggle to meet basic needs such as food and stability.

Ever wonder where your money really goes?

Many people hesitate to donate because they cannot see the real impact of their contributions. This visual reflects the frustration people feel when donations disappear without transparency.

Transparency Builds Trust

Clear reporting and visible impact help restore confidence in charitable giving. Showing measurable results encourages participation and helps donors understand the difference their contributions make.
